Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity in a nutshell is that "Neurons that fire together, wire together." which means that if you practice an activity it creates actual physical electrochemical pathways in your brain and those pathways can be strengthened by repetition. This is a huge breakthrough in the field of brain science as knowing this enable us to get better at an activity by just practicing regardless of your talent. A lot of people think that they cannot get good at something because they are not talented enough but it is actually not true. There are many studies researches which proves that just thinking about doing an activity makes neurons related to that activity stronger as much as physically doing that activity. When it comes to studying e.g coding just telling yourself that you can get good at coding as a professional programmer and with enough practice you can achieve that.

A lot of people especially students think you they are born with a set amount of ability and that they cannot improve beyond that, that is what physiologists call a fixed mindset. Human brains are capable of growing and creating more neuron network and there is no limit for that no matter your age, gender. A growth mindset is the opposite of above in which you don't limit yourself to amount of skills you have but instead you try to improve by learning and practicing practicing and knowing that your brain is capable of taking new information and becoming good at that activity.

I started to learn guitar last year and at the beginning I felt that I could not get better my abilities were the same even after practicing for a while and I was basically near to give up. However, continuing to practice for countless hours I started to see progress and my finger were automatically pressing the correct string by just thinking about the chorus. Know that I have learned about Neuroplasticity and growth mindset I can relate it back to my journey of learning to play guitar. Having this knowledge help me to apply them in practicing my coding skills and keep myself motivated when things get though during the bootcamp.
